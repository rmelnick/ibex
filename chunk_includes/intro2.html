For each sentence you'll decide to what degree it represents an EVENT (or activity) vs. a STATE OF AFFAIRS (or condition). For example ...

  • “The Great Earthquake of 1906 struck San Francisco just after 5 a.m.”
  • “Someone was rude to you.”
...are fairly clearly events, something specific that occurred, whereas...
  • “Lassie was a famous dog.”
  • “Drugs already existed to help recovering alcoholics abstain from alcohol.”
...describe states of affairs, rather than particular occurrences. This is not a simple either-or distinction; some sentences describe situations that are intermediate between pure events and pure states, for example:
  • “It was raining.”
  • “Eve regretted telling him anything at all.”
